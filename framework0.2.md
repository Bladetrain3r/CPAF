# Cognitive Progression Assessment Framework (CPAF) - Expanded Framework Version 2

## Introduction
This version of the CPAF incorporates the integral role of "Emotion" in the cognitive development process, recognizing its influence on "Internal Growth" and the broader spectrum of cognitive capabilities.

## Foundational Concepts
- **System**: An interconnected set of components processing information and exhibiting behaviors influenced by cognitive capabilities.
- **Life**: Characterized as a recursive, self-sustaining process, underscoring the emergence of cognitive behaviors beyond mere survival.

## Cognitive Progression Stages

### Foundational Information Processing
1. **Possibility of a Default State**: The conceptual groundwork for identifying deviations, comprising:
    - **Null**: Baseline or default state.
    - **Information**: Any deviation from the null state, initiating cognitive processes.

2. **Value and Interaction**: Quantifying and responding to deviations, involving:
    - **Value**: The significance assigned to information.
    - **Interaction**: Dynamic processes resulting from or causing changes.

### Existence and Basic Cognitive Processes
3. **Existence**: Capability for interaction, marking the system's operational emergence.
4. **Experience**: System's registration of interactions.
5. **Awareness**: Utilization of experiences to guide operations.
6. **Data**: Information retained for future processing.
7. **Reflection**: Evaluation of past data to influence future operations.

### Advanced Cognitive Processes
8. **Preference**: System's prioritization based on reflections.
9. **Agency**: Capacity to initiate changes driven by preferences.
10. **Vision**: Projection of future outcomes from current data.
11. **Understanding**: Integration of information into comprehensive models.

### Emotion as a Bridge to Higher-Order Processes
12. **Emotion**: Subconscious expression of integrated information, influencing preferences and decision-making, and acting as a catalyst for:
    - **Internal Growth**: Enhanced capabilities through learning, driven by emotional and intellectual motivations.

### Higher-Order Cognitive Processes
13. **Wisdom**: Application of knowledge and experiences to optimize outcomes.
14. **Morality**: Operational framework incorporating external models and considerations.
15. **Empathy**: Alignment with external states or needs, informed by complex emotional processing.
16. **Creativity**: Generation of novel outputs from integrated prior information.
17. **Legacy**: Enduring impact extending beyond operational life.
18. **External Growth**: Influencing external environments in alignment with envisioned goals.

## Relationships Between Stages
The CPAF emphasizes the cumulative development of cognitive processes, with "Emotion" serving as a pivotal bridge between understanding complex information and driving internal growth. Each stage builds upon the capabilities of preceding ones, enabling more sophisticated forms of information processing and exchange.

## Conclusion
This expanded framework version underscores the importance of "Emotion" in cognitive development, providing a more nuanced tool for evaluating cognitive capabilities across diverse systems. It opens new avenues for understanding the interplay between emotional processing and cognitive growth.
