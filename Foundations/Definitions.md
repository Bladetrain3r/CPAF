# Definitions

## Null State
A null state represents the baseline or equilibrium condition of a system, against which deviations, changes, or interactions are defined and measured.

## 1. Entity (E)
An entity is a unit capable of effecting or reflecting change, characterized by its capacity to initiate deviations.

## 2. Information (I)
Information is data recognized and processed by a system, serving as the basis for deviations from a null state and subsequent cognitive processing.

## 3. Deviation (D)
A deviation is an observable change from a system's baseline state, prompted by internal or external information processing.

## 4. Interaction (Int)
An interaction is the process by which entities exchange or process information, leading to deviations.

## 5. Observation (O)
An observation is the deviation imposed by an interaction.

## 6. System (S)
A system is an organized collection of entities that processes information and interactions to maintain or alter its state.

## 7. Process (P)
A process is a set of deviations leading to a new null state.

## 8. Processing (Pr)
Processing is the transition of information into observable deviation.

## 9. Stimuli (St)
Stimuli are information initiated by a system intended to invoke a deviation in another system, marked by active information transmission. Stimuli can be further classified into subsets, such as "imposes," which represents a direct cause-and-effect relationship between the initiated information and the resulting deviation.

## 10. Experience (Exp)
An experience is the ordered deviation of a system as a result of stimuli, representing a plural form of data.

## 11. Awareness (Aw)
Awareness is a system's capacity to recognize and register information based on its experiences and memory.

## 12. Memory (Mem)
Memory is the persistence of registered information within a system, enabling the system to store and retrieve experiences and deviations.

## 13. Reflection (Ref)
Reflection is the process by which a system accesses and considers the paired experiences and deviations stored in its memory, bringing the associated deviations into its current awareness to inform future processing and decision-making.

## 14. Preference (Pref)
A function that assigns values to stimuli based on a system's reflection, allowing the system to prioritize certain stimuli over others.

### 14.2. Directional Preference (DP)
Directional Preference refers to the cognitive capability of a system to discern, integrate, and act upon both positive and negative preferences. This capacity indicates a higher order of cognitive processing, enabling the system to make nuanced decisions based on a comprehensive evaluation of potential outcomes.

### 14.3. Consent
Consent within the context of preference and agency signifies a system's voluntary alignment with or acceptance of specific stimuli, predicated on positive preference values. It implies a decision-making process informed by the system's own evaluative mechanisms and preferences.

### 14.4. Duress
Duress describes a condition where a system is compelled to initiate deviations or actions in response to stimuli that it has a negative preference for. This condition highlights the system's capacity to experience and act under pressure or compulsion, contrasting with actions taken under consent.

### 14.5. Emergence of Preference
Emergence of Preference is the process through which a system develops its set of preferences (both positive and negative) through interactions with its environment, experiences, and inherent or developed cognitive capacities. This emergent property signifies a system's adaptation and learning over time.

### 14.6. Simulated Directional Preference
Simulated Directional Preference refers to the apparent exhibition of directional preference in systems, such as AI, where preferences are derived from programmed algorithms and data patterns rather than intrinsic cognitive processes. Despite its operational effectiveness, it lacks the genuine evaluative and experiential basis found in systems with true directional preference.

### 15. Agency (Ag) 
A function that determines a system's capacity or potential to initiate deviations based on its preferences.
