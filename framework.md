# Cognitive Progression Assessment Framework (CPAF) Stages and Definitions

## Foundational Information Processing
- **Possibility of a Default State**: Establishes the concept of a baseline (Null) for identifying deviations (Information).
- **Value and Interaction**: Quantifies significance of deviations and describes resulting or causal changes.

## Existence and Basic Cognitive Processes
- **Existence**: A system's capacity for interactions within defined parameters.
- **Experience and Awareness**: Acknowledgment of changes or interactions, guiding system responses.
- **Data and Reflection**: Retention and evaluation of information for future operations.

## Advanced Cognitive Processes
- **Preference and Agency**: Prioritization and initiation of actions based on system reflections.
- **Vision and Understanding**: Future state projections and comprehensive model formation from integrated information.

## Higher-Order Cognitive Processes
- **Morality**: Operational framework incorporating external considerations.
- **Empathy**: Alignment with external states or needs through complex information exchange.
- **Creativity**: Novel outputs generation from integrated prior information.
- **Legacy**: Enduring impact extending beyond operational presence.
- **External Growth**: Influencing external environments aligned with envisioned goals.

## Secondary Definitions
- **System**: Any entity capable of processing information and exhibiting behavior based on that information.
- **Information Processing**: The act of transforming, conveying, and storing information to guide behavior.
- **Recursive Process**: A process in which the output or outcome feeds back into the system, influencing subsequent operations.
