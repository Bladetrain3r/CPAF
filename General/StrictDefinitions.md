# CPAF Terminology Definitions

This document outlines the definitions of terms used within the Cognitive Progression Assessment Framework (CPAF). It serves as the canonical reference for understanding the precise meaning of each term in the context of the framework. In the spirit of "unix like" simplicity and specificity, each definition aims to convey a single, unambiguous meaning. When conflicts arise, this list holds precedence, and any discrepancies within the framework should be addressed in alignment with these definitions.

Foundational Concepts
Entity: The smallest unit within the framework that can initiate or represent change, consisting of discrete information with the potential to affect or create deviations.

Information: Structured or unstructured data that an entity processes, distinct from random noise by virtue of being recognizable and having potential relevance to the entity's state or behavior.

Deviation: A change from a previous or baseline state, identifiable by the system or entity as a result of processing information.

System: An organized collection of entities interacting in a manner that enables processing of information to achieve specific outcomes or behaviors. Systems are distinguished by their capacity to incorporate entities in a way that produces higher-order functions or processes.

## Atomic Stage Definitions
Each stage of the CPAF is defined atomically, focusing on its unique contribution to the cognitive process hierarchy.

### Foundations of Cognition

- **Value**: The significance or relevance assigned to information by the system, transforming deviations into quantifiable or qualitatively assessed inputs for decision-making processes.

- **Interaction**: The dynamic engagement of the system with information, leading to changes in state or behavior. Interaction encompasses the system's response to information and the subsequent effects of these responses within its environment or internal state.

- **System**: A set of distinct interactions

- **Existence**: The manifestation of a system capable of processing information and engaging in interactions. Existence implies the system's operational presence within a defined context, allowing for cognitive activities.

- **Experience**: The registration or acknowledgment of interactions by the system. Experience forms the basis for learning and adaptation, serving as the cumulative record of the system's engagements.

- **Awareness**: The system's capacity to detect, prioritize, and respond to information based on its current state of cognizance. Awareness enables the system to navigate its environment or context effectively.

- **Data**: Retained or recorded information within the system, which informs future processing, decision-making, or learning processes. Data represents the storage aspect of cognition, preserving experiences for subsequent reflection or analysis.

- **Reflection**: The evaluative process through which the system considers past actions, experiences, or data to influence or modify future behaviors or cognitive strategies. Reflection is a cornerstone of adaptive learning and decision-making.
