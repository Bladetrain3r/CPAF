Foundational Concepts
Entity: The smallest distinguishable unit capable of effecting or reflecting change, containing discrete information that can initiate deviations.

Information: Data recognized and processed by a system, serving as the basis for deviation from a null state and subsequent cognitive processing.
Identified by: A change

Deviation: Observable change from a system's baseline state, prompted by internal or external information processing.
Identified by: An information operation (i.e D = N + E(I))

System: An organized set of entities interacting to process information, exhibit behaviors, and achieve outcomes, characterized by recursive integration of processes.
Identified by: Composition

Emergence: The manifestation of a higher cognitive stage by a system as a direct consequence of the interaction and integration of preceding stages. 
Expressing the recursive and evolutionary nature of cognitive processes within the framework.
Identified by: Deviation absent stimuli

Simulation: The manifestation of cognitive capabilities as a direct response to external stimuli, without the autonomous generation of the cognitive process itself, such as by relying on pre-programmed patterns or data.
Identified by: Non-manifestation absent stimuli.

Foundational Definitions
Null: The default state of a system, serving as a reference point for identifying deviations.
Fundamentally identified by: Entropy

Stimuli: Information initiated by a system intended to invoke a deviation in another system, marked by active information transmission.
Fundamentally identified by: Information transfer

Lower Cognitive Segment
Experience: Accumulation of deviations and responses, forming the basis for a system's learning and future behavior adjustments.

Awareness: The system's capacity to detect, prioritize, and act on information relevant to its objectives or current state.

Data/Memory: Information retained from experiences, serving as a resource for informing present and future system operations.

Reflection: The system's process of evaluating past experiences to influence future decisions and behaviors.

Advanced Cognitive Processes
Preference: The system's selection of specific actions over others, shaped by its experiences and reflective processes.

Agency: The system's ability to autonomously initiate actions or decisions, driven by its preferences and reflective insights.

Vision: The system's projection of future states or outcomes based on its accumulated knowledge and current insights.

Understanding: The cohesive integration of information, enabling the system to navigate its environment or situations effectively.

Model: Cohesive integration of understanding into a representation of an external system, which can be used to inform decisions regarding that system.

Higher-Order Cognitive Processes
Internal Growth: Self-directed enhancement of the system's capabilities or structure, using experiences, reflections, and understanding for improvement. Internally driven improvement of models.

Wisdom: Deep application of knowledge and experience in decision-making, considering broader implications and long-term effects. Application of models to other models.

Morality: Actions and decisions aligned with internally consistent ethical principles, derived from the system's understanding and reflections.
Encapsulation of models into each other.

Empathy: The system's adaptive response to inferred states or needs of other entities, fostering beneficial interactions.
Simulation of the models of other systems.

Creativity: Novel outputs or ideas diverging from established norms, demonstrating the system's innovative use of information.
Creation of models that represent no existing external systems.

Legacy: Enduring influence of a system's actions or outputs, shaping future systems or the environment beyond its operational context.

Newly Integrated Definitions
Model: Structured data within a system representing an external system, derived from understanding and memory, facilitating the system's interaction with or simulation of external entities.
