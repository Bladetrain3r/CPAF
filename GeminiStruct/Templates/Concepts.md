### CPAF Concept File Format

1. **Title**
   - The name of the concept.

2. **Definition**
   - A concise, clear definition of the concept.

3. **Purpose and Relevance**
   - An explanation of why the concept is important within the framework.
   - How it connects to or interacts with other concepts.

4. **Examples**
   - Real-world or theoretical examples illustrating the concept.
   - Examples should demonstrate the concept's application and its impact on cognition or system dynamics.

5. **Properties and Characteristics**
   - A list or description of key properties and characteristics.
   - How these properties influence or manifest within systems or cognitive processes.

6. **Interactions and Relationships**
   - Details on how the concept interacts with other CPAF concepts.
   - The nature of these interactions and their implications for system behavior and cognition.

7. **Measurement and Quantification**
   - If applicable, how the concept can be measured or quantified.
   - Any standard units of measurement or assessment methods.

8. **Limitations and Exceptions**
   - Known limitations in the concept's applicability or explanatory power.
   - Exceptions to general rules or expectations.

9. **Further Reading and References**
   - Links to additional resources for deeper understanding.
   - References to foundational papers or texts.

10. **Revision History**
    - Record of updates or revisions to the concept file.
    - This section ensures the document remains a living resource that evolves over time.

### Example Entry: [Concept Title]

1. **Title:** Information

2. **Definition:** Information within a system is anything processable by an entity that, when processed, has the potential to induce a deviation from the baseline state.

3. **Purpose and Relevance:** Information is crucial for understanding system dynamics and cognitive processes. It serves as the fundamental trigger for change within systems.

...

This standardized format aims to ensure each concept file is comprehensive and informative, providing a clear understanding of its role within the CPAF. It allows for easy navigation among concepts and facilitates the identification of their interrelations and applications.
